# Effect of powder, binder and process parameters on anisotropic shrinkage in tape cast ceramic products

**Authors:** Andreas Heunisch, Armin Dellert, Andreas Roosen
**Journal:** Journal of the European Ceramic Society
**Year:** 2010

## Abstract

The study examines how powder morphology, binder type, and casting parameters influence anisotropic shrinkage in tape-cast alumina. Three alumina powders with distinct particle geometries (platelets, spherical, standard) and three polyvinyl butyral binders with varying molecular weights are compared.

## Key numeric findings

**Shrinkage anisotropy factor K_xy** (lateral-to-thickness ratio sensitivity proxy):
- Platelet powder (CL 3000): 12.7 average
- Standard powder (WRA FG): 8.4 average
- Spherical powder (DAW 05): 1.9 average

**In-plane lateral shrinkage:**
- Casting direction (x): 2.7–3.9 %
- Transverse direction (y): 3.0–4.0 %

**Thickness shrinkage:**
- 150 µm blade gap: ~11 % average
- 450 µm blade gap: ~6 % average

(Thickness shrinkage is significantly larger than in-plane shrinkage, and grows when blade gap is *smaller* — thinner tapes shrink more in thickness.)

**Green tape density:** 52–66 % of theoretical Al₂O₃ density.

**Standard slurry composition (wt%):**
- Al₂O₃ powder: 63.87 %
- Solvent (ethanol/toluol): 25.65 %
- Dispersant: 1.28 %
- Binder (PVB): 4.73 %
- Plasticizer: 4.47 %

**Particle orientation** (>50 % of particles oriented in casting direction across all samples):
- Platelet-based tapes: 70 % oriented
- Standard powder: 62 % oriented

**Binder molecular weights tested:**
- BL-1: 19 × 10³ g/mol
- B-98 (standard): 40–70 × 10³ g/mol
- B-74: 120–150 × 10³ g/mol

**Particle size:** d₅₀ = 3.8–4.2 µm for all three powders (so size is held roughly constant; the variable is shape).

**Casting parameters varied:** speed 10/50/250 mm/s; blade gap 150/450 µm.

## Key conclusion

**Particle morphology has the predominant effect on shrinkage anisotropy.** Binder molecular weight has only minor influence within the tested ranges. Casting speed and blade gap matter mostly through their effect on particle orientation and thickness, not through binder rheology.

## Implication for MLCC

For BaTiO₃ MLCC tapes:
- The dielectric powder shape (BME-grade BT is usually pseudo-spherical via hydrothermal synthesis or solid-state-milled with controlled aspect ratio) directly sets shrinkage anisotropy.
- The PVB binder choice matters less than the powder shape for shrinkage control.
- Thinner tapes shrink more in thickness than thicker tapes (relevant for ≤ 1 µm green layers in modern MLCCs).
