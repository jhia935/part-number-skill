# Effect of a BaTiO₃ nanoparticle additive on the quality of thin-film Ni electrodes in MLCC

**Authors:** Ken-ichi Sugimura (Noritake Kizai Co., Ltd.), Kazuhisa Hirao (Noritake Co., Ltd.)
**Journal:** *Journal of the Ceramic Society of Japan* 117 (2009) 1039–1043
**DOI:** 10.2109/jcersj2.117.1039

## Abstract

"To increase the film quality of the nickel electrode in a multi-layer ceramic capacitor (MLCC), the BaTiO₃ particles are usually used as an additive material in the electrode paste."

## Key quantitative data

| Parameter | Values |
|---|---|
| Ni powder size | ~0.2 µm |
| BaTiO₃ additive particle size | 30, 50, 100 nm |
| BaTiO₃ additive loading | 10–20 mass% |
| Optimal additive size | **30 nm** |
| Optimal loading | **10 mass%** |
| Resulting Ni film coverage | **> 75 %** |

## Key findings

1. **A polycrystalline BaTiO₃ coating layer on Ni** is highly effective at:
   - Protecting Ni from oxidation during cofiring
   - **Raising the shrinkage onset temperature of Ni** (suppresses early sintering of the electrode while the BT dielectric is still rigid)
2. **Particle-size sensitivity**: 30 nm BT additive at only 10 mass% reaches the same Ni-film coverage threshold (75 %) that 50–100 nm BT requires much more mass% to achieve. Smaller is much more effective per gram.
3. The role of the BT additive is **physical (constraint) rather than chemical** — it acts as a rigid second phase that retards Ni sintering, consistent with the Bordia-Scherer composite-sintering retardation framework and the Yan DEM simulations.

## Implications

- Matches the DEM-predicted **retardation factor of ~7× at 20 vol% / 60 nm BT** in Ni from [[yan-thesis-2013-mlcc-sintering-nanotomography|Yan thesis]].
- Industry consensus: nano-BT additive at 5–15 vol% (10–30 nm) is the standard recipe for Ni paste in BME MLCC.
- Higher loadings (> 20 mass%) hurt electrode conductivity by diluting the metal connectivity.
