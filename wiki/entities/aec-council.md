# Automotive Electronics Council (AEC)

A US-headquartered standards body that publishes the **AEC-Qxxx** family of automotive-grade qualification specs for electronic components. Founded in the 1990s by US automotive OEMs (Chrysler, Ford, GM); now international with all major automotive Tier 1s and component suppliers participating in the technical committees.

## Key documents
- **AEC-Q100** — ICs
- **AEC-Q101** — discrete semiconductors
- **AEC-Q104** — multi-chip modules
- **AEC-Q200** — passive electrical components ← **canonical reference for MLCCs**
- **AEC-Q005** — Pb-free test requirements

## AEC-Q200 governance
The Q200 sub-committee has representatives from:
- Automotive OEMs: BorgWarner, Bose, Continental, John Deere, Marelli, Robert Bosch, ZF
- MLCC vendors: [[murata]], [[tdk]], [[vishay]], [[yageo]]
- Other passive suppliers: Coilcraft, Diodes, Littelfuse, onsemi, Schurter, Harman
