# David (Donhang) Liu

Capacitor reliability specialist at **NASA Goddard Space Flight Center**, working under the NASA [[nasa-nepp]] (Electronic Parts and Packaging) program. Principal author of the BME MLCC reliability framework that synthesizes structure (`(r̄/d)^α`), stress (P-V acceleration + BME two-mode), and statistics (Weibull) into a single model.

## Key contributions
- **[[nasa-batio3-mlcc-failure-mechanisms]]** (CARTS 2012, Outstanding Paper Award) — identified "early failures" as extrinsic-defect driven (β > 1, not infant mortality), and proposed the geometric reliability factor `(1 − (r̄/d)^α)^N`. Quantified the **N-amplifier** effect that makes thinning dielectric layers actively dangerous unless per-layer reliability is astronomically high.
- **[[nasa-nepp-bme-mlcc-reliability]]** (EEE Parts Bulletin 2013) — full synthesis paper with the Table II case-size geometry data used industry-wide as a reference for BME chip dimensions / margins.
- Series of CARTS papers 2005–2013 on accelerated testing, failure analysis, and BME qualification for NASA space-level applications.

Contact (as published, 2013): NASA Goddard, 301-286-8573, donhang.liu-1@nasa.gov.

## Relevance
Liu's work is the **default citation** for any quantitative MLCC reliability claim in this knowledge base, and the basis of [[bme-reliability-model]].
