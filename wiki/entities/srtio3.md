# Strontium Titanate (SrTiO₃)

A cubic perovskite oxide, **quantum paraelectric** (no ferroelectric transition above 0 K, but very close — incipient ferroelectric). Used in MLCC dielectrics primarily as a **shift agent** for BaTiO₃-based Class II compositions and as a base material for some high-εr / linear hybrid dielectrics.

## Crystal & dielectric properties
- Cubic perovskite at room temperature, εr ≈ 300 (room T), rising sharply at low T due to incipient ferroelectricity.
- No ferroelectric phase transition in standard MLCC operating range.
- Wide band gap, generally reduction-tolerant in mild-reducing BME firing.

## Role in MLCC formulations
1. **Curie-point modulation in BaTiO₃**: Sr substitutes for Ba in BaTiO₃, lowering the Curie temperature. (Ba_x Sr_{1−x})TiO₃ ("BST") solid solutions tune T_c from ~125 °C down toward room temperature and below. Used in tunable RF capacitors and some specialty MLCC formulations.
2. **Hybrid Class II formulations** for low-DC-bias derating: e.g., (Ba, Sr)TiO₃ + Bi(Mg, Ti)O₃ + NaNbO₃ ceramics show **reduced temperature dependence and DC-bias sensitivity** simultaneously.
3. **Reliability physics reference material**: Waser et al.'s foundational studies of oxygen-vacancy electromigration were on SrTiO₃ single crystals and ceramics ([[nasa-ir-degradation-ni-batio3-2015]]).
