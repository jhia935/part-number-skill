# NASA NEPP (Electronic Parts and Packaging)

A NASA-funded program that continuously evaluates electronic-component reliability for space-flight use. Headquartered at Goddard Space Flight Center; cross-center participation including JPL.

## Relevance to MLCC research
- NEPP funds the **most quantitative open BME-MLCC reliability work** available — primarily through [[donhang-liu]]'s decade-long study (2004–2013+).
- NEPP publishes "Selection, Qualification, Inspection, and Derating of Multilayer Ceramic Capacitors with Base-Metal Electrodes" (NASA NEPP FY Report 2013) — the de-facto government-grade derating manual.
- The MIL-PRF-123 reliability spec historically required PME ([[bme-vs-pme]]) MLCCs for space; the NEPP program is the technical basis for the careful BME adoption now underway.
