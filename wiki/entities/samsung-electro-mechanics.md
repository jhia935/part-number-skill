# Samsung Electro-Mechanics (SEMCO)

The largest Korean MLCC manufacturer, Suwon HQ. Second-place to [[murata]] in global MLCC market share.

## Product portfolio
- **CL series** ([[samsung-cl-series]]) — general-purpose, the SEMCO equivalent of [[murata-grm-series]]. Codes 03/05/10/21/31/32/43/55 mapping to EIA 0201–2220. Class I (C0G plus the full P/R/S/T/U TCC family) and Class II/III (X5R/X7R/X6S/Y5V). Voltage range 4 V to 3000 V.
- **CB series** — soft-termination / flex-mitigation.
- AEC-Q200 automotive grade lineup (see SEMCO's MLCC_Automotive January 2024 catalog).

## Patent activity
US 11,211,181 / 11,295,894 / 11,763,990 / 11,776,748 / 12,488,941 — BaTiO₃-based dielectric compositions with Tb-Dy co-doping (1.2–3.0 mol% Tb and 2.5–3.4 mol% Dy per 100 mol BaTiO₃) and other lanthanide rare earths. Patenting in the rare-earth dopant ratio space puts SEMCO directly in competition with Murata's WO2024247128A1.

## Materials-process gap (as of public reporting)
Industrial reporting (e.g., MLCC supercycle analyses) describes Korean BaTiO₃ powder particle size around 300–500 nm vs Murata's 100 nm — a real gap in raw-material control that constrains the smallest dielectric layer SEMCO can build reliably. SEMCO's patent activity around rare-earth doping is partly aimed at closing this through chemistry rather than mechanical-grinding.
