# Sodium Niobate (NaNbO₃)

A perovskite niobate with a complex polymorphism — exhibits both **antiferroelectric** and **ferroelectric** phases depending on temperature, composition, and grain size. Used in MLCC formulations to extend temperature stability into X8R / X9R territory and as a base material for **lead-free** high-T dielectrics replacing PLZT/PZT.

## Crystal & dielectric properties
- Orthorhombic at room T (antiferroelectric P phase); transitions through ferroelectric and paraelectric phases above ~370 °C.
- εr ≈ 200–500 in pure form; can be much higher in solid solutions.
- Lead-free alternative for high-temperature stable Class II dielectric (X8R and beyond) and for **antiferroelectric energy-storage capacitors**.

## Role in MLCC formulations
- **X9R-class dielectric**: NaNbO₃-modified BaTiO₃-BiMg₁/₂Ti₁/₂O₃ ceramics with stable εr from −55 to +200 °C (research-grade).
- **DC-bias-tolerant Class II**: (Ba, Sr)TiO₃ + Bi(Mg, Ti)O₃ + NaNbO₃ ceramics show low DC-bias VCC effect and broadened temperature stability.
- **Lead-free piezoelectric (KNN, K₁/₂Na₁/₂NbO₃)** — base for lead-free piezoceramic-on-MLCC actuators; not strictly capacitor dielectric, but uses the same BME co-firing process and shares the rare-earth doping playbook.
