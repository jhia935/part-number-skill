# Dynamic Microscopic Structures and Dielectric Response in the Cubic-to-Tetragonal Phase Transition for BaTiO₃

**Source:** `resources/literature/arxiv-batio3-cubic-to-tetragonal-md.md` (PDF: `resources/literature/pdf/arxiv-batio3-cubic-to-tetragonal-md.pdf`)
**Venue:** arXiv:1010.6147, 2010

## Summary

A first-principles molecular-dynamics study of the cubic↔tetragonal phase transition in BaTiO₃ at T_C ≈ 393 K (120 °C). Computes the dynamic structures and dielectric response across the transition.

## Key findings

- Transition is first-order with small thermal hysteresis.
- Above T_C: cubic Pm-3m, no net polarization but **strong soft-phonon fluctuations** (lattice instabilities); the dielectric response near T_C is dominated by these soft modes.
- Below T_C: tetragonal P4mm, off-center Ti⁴⁺ displacement, six C₄-axis polar variants.
- The transition is **not a simple symmetry break** — there's a polar precursor region above T_C where dipoles fluctuate locally but average to zero. This is what drives the diverging εr at T_C.

## Connection to MLCC physics

1. **εr peak at T_C ≈ 9000–15 000** is the soft-mode signature; engineered Class II compositions broaden and flatten this peak via doping.
2. **Domain pattern on cooldown** depends on stress / grain-size constraints — see [[cubic-tetragonal-transition]].
3. **Aging clock reset** above T_C is a literal "structural amnesia" — the lattice is genuinely cubic, no domains exist; on cooling fresh tetragonal nucleation produces a random domain configuration.
