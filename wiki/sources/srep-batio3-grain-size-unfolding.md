# Sci. Rep. — Unfolding Grain Size Effects in BaTiO₃ Ferroelectric Ceramics

**Source:** `resources/literature/srep-batio3-grain-size-unfolding.md` (PDF: `resources/literature/pdf/srep-batio3-grain-size-unfolding.pdf`)
**Venue:** *Scientific Reports* (Nature) 9953, 2015
**Type:** Open-access experimental study

## Summary

Cross-method experimental survey of how grain size affects dielectric, piezoelectric, and ferroelectric properties in BaTiO₃ ceramics. Key contribution: shows that the **dielectric permittivity vs grain size relationship is nearly independent of the sintering method or starting powder used**, while piezoelectric d₃₃ and remnant polarization Pᵣ do depend on processing — clarifying which observations are "intrinsic" to grain size and which are processing artifacts.

## Key findings

### Dielectric permittivity
- **Peak εᵣ at grain size ~ 0.8–1.1 µm** — robust across sintering methods (conventional vs spark plasma).
- Below the peak, εᵣ **rapidly decreases** with decreasing grain size.
- At 50 nm grain: εᵣ falls **below 1000**.
- Mechanism: combination of intrinsic size effect (Landau-Ginzburg) plus "dead layer" dilution at grain boundaries.

### Piezoelectric d₃₃ and remnant polarization Pᵣ
- These properties have **method-dependent** grain-size relationships — unlike εᵣ.
- For ceramics from larger (micro-) starting powders, d₃₃ and Pᵣ **increase** with grain size.
- For nano-starting powders, the behavior diverges.
- Identifies the **domain-wall contribution** as the unifying factor.

### Critical size for ferroelectricity
- Confirms 10–20 nm critical size for ferroelectricity disappearance in **sintered ceramic** form.
- Larger (10–30 nm) than in some thin-film studies because the polycrystalline grain-boundary network provides additional constraint.

### Tetragonality (c/a)
- c/a drops from ~ 1.011 at 5 µm grain to **1.000 (essentially cubic) at 100 nm**.
- Direct structural evidence of the ferroelectric-to-paraelectric crossover.

## Why this paper matters for MLCC

The thin-layer state of the art is right at the threshold of the εᵣ collapse:
- 0.3 µm dielectric → r̄ ≤ 100 nm → εᵣ ~ 1000 region
- 0.5 µm dielectric → r̄ ≤ 167 nm → εᵣ ~ 1500–2000 region
- 1 µm dielectric → r̄ ≤ 333 nm → εᵣ ~ 3000–4000 region

This is the physical reason **thin-layer scaling has limits**: as you push r̄ smaller to fit more grains in a thinner layer, εᵣ falls and you give back the volumetric efficiency gain.

[[core-shell-batio3|Core-shell engineering]] is the industrial response: the **core** stays at 100–200 nm to preserve ferroelectricity, while the engineered **shell** replaces the uncontrolled "dead layer" with a controlled paraelectric layer.

## Methodology highlights
- Compared conventional sintering, spark plasma sintering, and two-step sintering routes.
- Range tested: 0.3–3.5 µm dense BaTiO₃ ceramics.
- Single-method measurement removes most of the cross-study artifacts that plague the older grain-size-vs-εᵣ literature.
