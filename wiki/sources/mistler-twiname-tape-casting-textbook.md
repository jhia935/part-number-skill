# Mistler & Twiname — Tape Casting: Theory and Practice (2000)

**Authors:** Richard E. Mistler, Eric R. Twiname
**Publisher:** American Ceramic Society / Wiley, 1st edition 2000 (ISBN 978-1574980295), ~298 pages
**Note:** Reference page only — physical book; not ingested as raw text. The book is the **standard practitioner reference** for tape casting in the ceramic industry and is cited throughout the MLCC literature.

## Why it matters here

Tape casting is the unit operation that produces MLCC green sheets. Almost everything about post-sinter dimensional control, anisotropic shrinkage, density uniformity, and lamination quality traces back to choices made at this stage:
- Slurry rheology and dispersant chemistry
- Binder/plasticizer choice and ratios
- Solvent system (aqueous vs solvent-based)
- Doctor-blade gap, casting speed, drying profile

## Chapter outline (from publisher / TOC sources)

1. Introduction and history
2. Materials technology and selection
3. Materials processing: slip preparation
4. The tape casting process
5. Further tape processing (drying, blanking, lamination)
6. Applications of tape technology
7. Water-based (aqueous) processing

The book covers both **the handbook-style how-to** (slurry formulation tables, casting parameter ranges, troubleshooting) and **system-level discussion** (how each variable couples).
