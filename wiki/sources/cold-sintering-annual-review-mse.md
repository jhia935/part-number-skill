# Guo, Randall et al. — Cold Sintering Progress, Challenges, Future Opportunities

**Source:** `resources/literature/cold-sintering-annual-review-mse.md` (PDF: `resources/literature/pdf/cold-sintering-annual-review-mse.pdf`)
**Authors:** Jing Guo, Richard Floyd, Sarah Lowum, Jon-Paul Maria, Thomas Herisson de Beauvoir, Joo-Hwan Seo, Clive A. Randall (Penn State Materials Research Institute)
**Venue:** *Annual Review of Materials Research* 49 (2019) 275–295

## Summary

The authoritative review of [[cold-sintering|cold sintering]] — densification of ceramic powders at room temperature to ~300 °C using a transient liquid phase and uniaxial pressure. Authored by the Randall group that pioneered the technique 2016+. Surveys mechanisms (multi-stage liquid-phase-sintering-like model), demonstrated material systems, activation-energy values for grain growth (lower than thermal sintering), and future directions.

## Why it matters for MLCC
Not directly applicable to current production MLCC (pressure incompatible with internal electrodes; density not high enough). But points to a **future path** where ceramic-polymer composite MLCCs become possible, with the dielectric cold-sintered around polymer-based "electrodes" — a wholly different manufacturing branch.

The review is also the **best modern primer on transient-liquid densification physics** that informs even conventional [[sintering-aids-glass|sintering aid]] design.
