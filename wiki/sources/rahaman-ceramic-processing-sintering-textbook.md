# Rahaman — Ceramic Processing and Sintering (textbook)

**Author:** Mohamed N. Rahaman (Missouri University of Science and Technology)
**Books:**
- *Ceramic Processing and Sintering*, 2nd ed., CRC Press / Routledge, 2003 (ISBN 978-0824709884) — comprehensive process+sintering volume.
- *Sintering of Ceramics*, CRC Press, 2007 (ISBN 978-0849372865) — sintering-focused extraction from the 2003 book, expanded.
- *Ceramic Processing*, 2nd ed., CRC Press, 2017 — companion processing volume.

**Note:** Reference page only — physical books; not ingested as raw text. These are the **standard graduate-textbook references** for ceramic sintering science and are cited by both the [[yan-thesis-2013-mlcc-sintering-nanotomography|Yan thesis]] (ref [43]) and most MLCC literature.

## Why it matters here

Rahaman's books are the canonical treatment of:
- **Sintering thermodynamics and kinetics** — surface-energy reduction as driving force, the three stages (initial, intermediate, final), the Coble/Herring/Kingery models.
- **Master Sintering Curve** ([[master-sintering-curve]]) — Hansen-Su-Johnson framework derived here in detail.
- **Constrained sintering of composites, films, and multilayers** — dedicated chapters in both books on Bordia-Scherer-Hsueh-Scherer-Garino models, inclusion-induced retardation, camber/warpage of multilayers.
- **Green-body forming** — tape casting, slip casting, dry pressing as competing routes.
