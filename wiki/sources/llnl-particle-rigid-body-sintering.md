# LLNL — Particle Rigid-Body Motion During Sintering

**Source:** `resources/literature/llnl-particle-rigid-body-sintering.md` (PDF: `resources/literature/pdf/llnl-particle-rigid-body-sintering.pdf`)
**Publisher:** Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory
**Document:** LLNL-JRNL-820428

## Summary

A computational / experimental study of how individual ceramic particles **move and rearrange** as rigid bodies during sintering — translation, rotation, and tilt at the early-stage densification phase. Complements the classical mass-transport view of sintering ([[sintering-kinetics-batio3]]): in the **first** stage of sintering, particles aren't yet bonded into a rigid framework, so they can mechanically rearrange under capillary forces and any applied pressure, in addition to the diffusional neck-growth picture.

## Relevance to MLCC
- The pre-sintering stage (600–1100 °C ramp) in [[sintering-profile-mlcc|MLCC sintering profiles]] is exactly where rigid-body particle motion is most active.
- Affects ultimate microstructure homogeneity: agglomerated regions of the green body can rearrange differently from well-dispersed regions, leading to density gradients post-sintering.
- Important for [[constrained-sintering-mlcc|constrained sintering]] models: in-plane constraint can suppress particle rearrangement, contributing to anisotropic shrinkage.
